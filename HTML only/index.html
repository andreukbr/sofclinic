The Proposal: Summary

The solution-focused clinic will comprise 4-6 sessions which will be offered as the client progresses to Outpatient Clinic (OPC) or to Primary Care (EPCL - Extended Primary Care Liaison).

It can also be offered to client as an alternative to care-coordination when OPC or EPCL client is in need of further input (frequently referred as "a short-term period of care-coordination")

Eligibility

Clients who are ready to be discharged, i.e.:

  • not in crisis:
    • no admission within the last year;
    • no A&E attendance leading to admission during the last 3 months;

    • AND

  • not accruing outcomes from care-coordination:
    • not interested on care-coordination intervention;
    • not accruing recovery outcomes

    •  

    Exclusion:

    Care-coordinated Clients who meet any of the following criteria:

    1. Clients in need of intensive input support (once weekly or longer) and issues demand too much resources from Duty system;
    2. Clients admitted for more than one week during the last six months;
    3. Clients who are more likely to benefitting from Care-coordination than from SF approach

Hypothesis / Expected outcomes

  • There will be an increase in progress of clients towards Primary Care;
  • There will be a decrease of flow of clients from Primary Care to Secondary Care;
  • There will be a decrease in time-length of care-coordination,i.e. clients will be capable to take over their care quickly than currently;
  • Clients will become more active participants on their own care.

Intervention

Solution Focused Clinic will be run as per Brief’s Solution Focused Practice Manual:

PRINCIPLES:

  • Problem-free talk (“What are your best hopes” rather than “how can I help you?”;
  • A description of their future without the problem (“Miracle question”)
  • Scales:
    • Progress already made;
    • Future signs of progress;
    • Identification of Exceptions
      • What makes it to be 3/10 and not 0/10;
      • Description of exceptional moments when “pieces of the miracle” are already occurring;

SESSIONS

PRE-SESSION: Wrapping UP the problem talk.

 - Review DIALOG+;

- Review Care Plan and Crisis Plan;

- Review

SESSION ONE (It may occur on the second part of the pre-session):

  1. Identify the Client Best hopes (What the client hopes for that one session);
  2. A description of the Future without the problem;
  3. Exceptions: pieces of that preferred future already occurring in the present;
  4. pre-break: ask if there is anything else the client wants to discuss before feedback;
  5. Break: Documentation of:

                Dialog+;

                Miracle answers;

                Exceptions Answers;

                Resources.

  1. Feedback and Suggestions:

 - constructive/positive summary/feedback

- Suggestions:

                 Client to notice any improvement within the next week(s)

                client to notice what (s)he is doing when up the scale

                Client to notice what it is done to resist arguing/being violent/drinking etc.

 

SECOND SESSION AND FOLLOW-UPS

Monitoring of the above under a SF framework


LAST SESSION:

  • To reflect over the process;
  • To book follow-up in 4-6 months

 

FOLLOW-UP SESSION:

DIALOG+ follow-up

CSRI (Client Service Receipt Inventory) follow-up.

What is the quality issue you want to work on?

  • Caseload makes personalised care impracticable;
  • Most care-coordination work has been relying on monitoring client’s mental state, when in fact this could be done in more creative ways;
  • to give client control back over his recovery –empowering clients to coordinate their own carerather them having an external agency (care coordinator) to do it.
  • To check if there was any increase in recovery outcomes for the client during the last 6 months prior discharge;
  • To make a retrospective analysis to conclude that a certain client could have been discharged six months earlier;




What is the issue/problem?

  • Transition from care-coordination to OPC often trunked by uncertainty:

“Are the clients ready to coordinate their own care?”

  • In consequence, client remains on caseload for further 6 months (i.e. until next CPA) with few to nil further accrual of Recovery Capital.
  • Same dilemma present on OPC:

“Certain clients need further input… but, do they need long-term care-coordination?”

 

Why is this* a problem ?

  • Waste of resource
    • Care-coordination works in a spectrum which is proportionally inverse to the size of caseload;
    • See Appendix 1: Care coordinators are obliged:
      • to prioritise between core duties, since it is virtually impossible to undertake all tasks that are expected from them;
      • to define how much input will be given to clients;
      • to level down quality of reports, since they end up left to last minute;
      • to set aside personal development;
    • Consequence:
      • Less time spent with clients;
      • Low morale-motivation
        • Effect on retention;
        • Effect on absenteeism;

 

How will you know that a change is an improvement? What are your measures?

The flow of clients from Care-co to OPC will be more evident and more logical/measurable;

The flow of clients from Care-co to OPC will be more evident and more logical/measurable;

                The above will be measured through analysis of data collected as follows:

  • Number of clients allocated to SF clinic
  • Number of clients discharged from SF clinic to OPC;
  • Number of clients discharged from SF clinic to EPCL;
  • Number of clients discharged from SF back to OPC (i.e. prevented care-coordination);
